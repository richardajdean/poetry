This cold green stone

This cold green stone

Moss soaked

Shares a colour with none of the leaves

The grass here lives half a life

Waiting to be flattened


A ship trying to sink

Anchored above ground

Days crossed off

Scratched in rain

Wiped clean by the morning


Rolling this stone away

Would take more men than I know

Stronger too

And what would be revealed?

Bare shrinking earth

The worms chewing through it
