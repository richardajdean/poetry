Richard Dean

Back

Haiku from a house near Dartmoor

The axe is still and
He pulls his arms down hard and
Logs are split spinning

In here no leaves move
Flies push their way through thick air
Hot light trapped in glass

An escaped bull takes
No notice of the shouting
As he runs downhill

The sky is spilt milk
A sheep lies on the hillside
Is it dead is it

Bracken sits steaming
Ripples stir the long drowned grass
The buzzards scream here

When I was told my
Father shot and killed rabbits
I felt joy and fear

Rock shaped by the wind
Rock shaped by river water
Rock shaped by a man

A heron woven
Into the brocade of reeds
Trees sing sibilance

Sun drips from the trees
Filling invisible pools
We walk between them

Pyrite flecks of car
Glint away from and toward
The hidden valley
